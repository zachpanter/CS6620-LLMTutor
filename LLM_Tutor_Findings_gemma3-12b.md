The phrase "The obstacle is the way" isn't about magically making problems disappear. It’s about *how* you respond to them. Marcus Aurelius believed that difficulties, setbacks, and adversities aren't inherently bad. In fact, they present opportunities for growth, virtue, and practice. They are, in a sense, a *path* to becoming a better person.

Think of it like this: a sculptor needs a block of marble. The marble itself isn’t a masterpiece, but the sculptor’s skill is revealed *through* working with the marble, overcoming its imperfections, and shaping it into something beautiful.  The marble, the ‘obstacle’, *is* the way to the sculpture.
